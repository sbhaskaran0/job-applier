Please share what you did day-to-day and what you created/built.
In my role as a Product Strategy Associate at M Science, I initiated, led, and executed on high-value strategic projects and overhauled internal processes and systems. Though the day-to-day varied, my role was to be the ultimate generalist, breaking down the strategic goals into actionable, executable steps, and to execute on those projects with whatever tools, resources, and collaborators I could muster and align stakeholders on.
One example was building a master data system for product metadata--I identified that a significant point of internal friction was that the organization lacked a clear understanding or definition of what our "product" even was; different teams grouped and bucketed our output differently, leading to siloed work, confusing processes and no ability to properly sell our work as M Science moved away from enterprise deals.
I was able to work with key stakeholders to create a source of truth on product definitions and our explicit product offerings, and associate all our disparate systems in a single interoperable platform, allowing my work to be leveraged in key business systems including but not limited to our customer portal, the semantic layer of of our AI product, product entitlements, unit economics, and our internal analytics, all of which I either built directly or worked closely with key stakeholders to make.
