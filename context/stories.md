# Stories & talking points

Reusable, concrete examples for open-ended application questions. Each has a
situation → action → measurable result the agent can adapt.

## Driving revenue through product (API schema)
At M Science I owned the KPI API product, which delivers forecasts of critical
KPIs for hundreds of companies to institutional/quant investors. Through
discovery I found two blockers to adoption by quant funds: the API's restatement
structure created look-ahead bias that invalidated their models, and clients
lacked the semantic context to use the data. I worked with engineering to design
a new schema that solved both — a point-in-time history methodology for
restatements, standardized dimension tables for company/metric metadata (built
with our investment research team), and external IDs linking to standard industry
datasets. Ahead of launch I worked with sales and marketing leadership on the
GTM, and the release drove a steep increase in new-business deals — doubling the
number of quant teams using the data and ~80% ARR growth for the product.

## Improving quality with ML (anomaly detection + QA)
I built ML anomaly detection and an OCR/LLM-based QA layer for the KPI product,
reducing the annual error rate from over 2% to roughly 0% — a step change in the
reliability clients depend on for investment decisions.

## Pricing & packaging strategy
As Product Strategy Associate I led development of the packaging and pricing
model, working across data science, sales, and investment research to make it
data-driven, and stood up a pricing desk. ARR rose ~35% in the first quarter
after rollout.

## Building internal platforms (Salesforce master-data system)
I identified that a major source of internal friction was that M Science lacked a
clear, shared definition of what our "product" even was — different teams bucketed
our output differently, which caused siloed work, confusing processes, and no
clean way to package and sell our work as we moved away from enterprise deals. I
worked with key stakeholders to create a single source of truth on product
definitions and offerings, and conceptualized and led adoption of an internal
Salesforce product as the master data system for all product metadata — a
semantic layer for AI products that automated entitlements, powered the customer
portal, and enabled product-level unit economics and analytics.

## Hardest problem — automating customer entitlements
The hardest I've worked was building the automated entitlements system above.
It replaced a manual process that consumed 40+ hours a week, but standing it up
required a huge amount of manual validation and transformation to populate the
data. I first attacked the scope: I reached out to data science and engineering
to find what could be automated or optimized, then personally wrote a Python
script for the complex mapping to cut down the records needing review. Once I'd
exhausted the automation options, I led a 3-person team through two weeks of
focused manual validation, personally reviewing and correcting over 10,000
records. My takeaway: operations professionals succeed by knowing which work can
be simplified or automated and which genuinely requires doing the difficult,
undesirable work by hand — and then doing it.

## Why product management
I like sitting at the intersection of data, strategy, and execution: turning
messy inputs into a clear roadmap, aligning cross-functional teams, and shipping
things that move measurable business outcomes. My background in data/analytics
lets me ground product decisions in evidence. I've built my career on seeking
out and solving big problems, and I'm most drawn to products that solve a real,
tangible problem for their users — I want to leverage the strategic
problem-solving I've done in high-growth, technically focused environments on a
product I can feel genuinely passionate about.
