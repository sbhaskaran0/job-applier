# Stories & talking points

Reusable, concrete examples for open-ended application questions. Each has a
situation → action → measurable result the agent can adapt.

## Driving revenue through product (API schema)
At M Science I owned the KPI API product. I worked with engineering to define
and launch a new API schema that exposed additional metadata endpoints and
improved interoperability with industry-standard datasets. This directly
increased product ARR by ~80% by making the product easier to adopt alongside
clients' existing data stacks.

## Improving quality with ML (anomaly detection + QA)
I built ML anomaly detection and an OCR/LLM-based QA layer for the KPI product,
reducing the annual error rate from over 2% to roughly 0% — a step change in the
reliability clients depend on for investment decisions.

## Pricing & packaging strategy
As Product Strategy Associate I led development of the packaging and pricing
model, working across data science, sales, and investment research to make it
data-driven, and stood up a pricing desk. ARR rose ~35% in the first quarter
after rollout.

## Building internal platforms (Salesforce master-data system)
I conceptualized and led adoption of an internal Salesforce product as the master
data system for all product metadata — a semantic layer for AI products that
automated entitlements, powered the customer portal, and enabled product-level
unit economics and analytics.

## 0→1 product work (fractional PM)
For a B2B2C personal-finance startup I led discovery, defined the ICP and user
personas, set the strategy and roadmap, and used AI prototyping tools to produce
wireframes — resulting in an MVP and a clear product vision, plus Agile/CI-CD
practices for the team.

## Operational improvement (report publishing)
At YipitData I analyzed the report-publishing process and product-team
interviews, proposed new best practices, and implemented them — cutting delayed
reports by 50% and freeing analyst time.

## Why product management
I like sitting at the intersection of data, strategy, and execution: turning
messy inputs into a clear roadmap, aligning cross-functional teams, and shipping
things that move measurable business outcomes. My background in data/analytics
lets me ground product decisions in evidence.

## Strengths
Data-driven product strategy, executive communication, cross-functional
alignment, and comfort going deep technically (SQL/Python/ETL/AI-ML) while
owning commercial outcomes (ARR, pricing, GTM).
