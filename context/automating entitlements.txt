What is the hardest you have every worked on something, what was it, and why?
The Hardest I ever worked was on a project to build a new entitlements system, where I was responsible for taking a manual process that took 40+ man-hours a week and building an automated process to power our customer entitlements. Though the work itself would save the company countless hours, building the system would require a tremendous investment of manual validation and transformation to populate the data.
To accomplish this goal, I sought out ways to cut down the scope of work, first by reaching out to data science and engineering colleagues to investigate ways to automate or optimize, then by personally writing a Python script to do complex mapping and cut down the number of records to review.
Once all avenues had been either exhausted or implemented to reduce scope, I led a team of 3 resources to do the manual validation work over the period of 2 weeks of dedicated attention. It is my belief that operations professionals are most successful when they learn to identify which work can be simplified and automated, and which work requires doing difficult, undesirable work; having determined that this work was necessary to do manually, I reviewed and corrected over 10,000 records.
